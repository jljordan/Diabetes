"One day, I was driving to work and I could't tell what color the traffic light was. Straight lines became wavy. Objects disappeared from my sight. I felt like I was in a Harry Potter movie, like when the bus lengthens and shortens. I couldn't read or distinguish letters. One day I was at the grocery store when I began seeing strobing lights which got worse and worse until I was afraid to move. I am being treated now, and I am fortunate that medication is available to restore some degree of sight for people with Macula Edema, but I still have to deal with monthly injections in each of my eyes and eventually cataract surgery when the Retinopathy is sufficiently under control..."

"Type 1 Diabetic for 30+ years (I am 33 now). At the age of 25 I lost my eye sight due to retinal detachments in both eyes (diabetic retinopathy). I have had 14+ eye surgeries to repair my vision (with some success) I currently do not work/drive due to my vision impairment I am however able to live independently and raise my 8 year old daughter on my own. T1D is a HORRIBLE disease to live with...you have to fight it 24/7 365 you get no breaks, no vacations from this...but the key is for YOU to control it, don't ever let it control YOU!!"

i was diagnosed with Type 2 Diabetes in 1999, but I know I've had it at least 15 or 20 years before that. I have been able to control my blood-sugar levels most of the time, but as I get older, it is harder for me to control. I now can't eat many of the things that I used to enjoy without my tests reading way too high. I am on Glipizide and Metformin prescriptions. I take a 500 mg. Metformin before I eat a meal. I don't eat large meals, but I try to eat small portions and eat more often. and I also take a Glipizide 10 mg. with the Metformin if I eat starchy, or sweet things. My readings should stay between 100 and 120, but one of my doctors told me to keep it under 150. I have to try harder now to do that.
